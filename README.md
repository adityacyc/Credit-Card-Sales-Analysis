# Credit Card Financial Dashboard

## Project Overview

The Credit Card Financial Dashboard is a data analytics project designed to analyze customer spending behavior, transaction patterns, fraud activities, credit utilization, and customer engagement. The dashboard transforms raw transactional data into actionable business insights, enabling financial institutions to make informed decisions related to customer retention, risk management, fraud detection, and revenue optimization.

Developed using Tableau, the dashboard provides an interactive and comprehensive view of credit card performance through key performance indicators, trend analysis, customer segmentation, and predictive forecasting.

---

## Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to:

* Monitor customer spending patterns across different categories.
* Analyze transaction volumes and customer engagement.
* Identify fraudulent transactions and high-risk customer segments.
* Evaluate customer activity across digital channels.
* Detect overspending behavior and potential credit risks.
* Forecast future spending and fraud trends.
* Support data-driven decision-making for financial institutions.

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains credit card transaction records and customer-related information, including:

* Customer ID
* Credit Card ID
* Customer Segment
* Credit Limit
* Transaction Value
* Transaction Date
* Activity Channel
* Card Family
* Fraud Indicators
* Customer Activity Status
* RFM Metrics (Recency, Frequency, Monetary Value)

The dataset was cleaned, standardized, and transformed to ensure consistency and accuracy for analysis.

## Key Insights

* A small number of customer segments contribute significantly to total revenue.
* Digital channels account for a substantial portion of customer transactions.
* Certain card families exhibit higher spending and fraud exposure.
* Overspending behavior can be identified through transaction monitoring.
* Fraud activity shows identifiable patterns that can support proactive risk management.
* Forecasting models provide valuable insights into future spending and fraud trends.
